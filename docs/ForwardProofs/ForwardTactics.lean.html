Forward Reasoning with Tactics

Many users, including most of the mathematicians that form the majority of Lean’s user community, prefer the tactic mode. But even in tactic mode, it can be useful to reason in a forward fashion, mixing forward and backward reasoning steps.

The structured proof commands have, let, and calc are also available as tactics, making this possible.

have

have [[name :] proposition] :=
    proof

The have tactic lets us state and prove an intermediate lemma in tactic mode. Afterwards, the lemma is available as a hypothesis in the goal state.

let

let name [: type] := term

The let tactic lets us introduce a local definition in tactic mode. Afterwards, the defined symbol and its definition are available in the goal state.

The calc tactic lets us enter a calculational proof in tactic mode. The tactic has the same syntax as the structured proof command of the same name. We can regard apply calc . . . , where calc is a tactic, as an alias for apply (exact calc . . .), where calc is the structured proof command described above.
